ESG Trends for Small Businesses in 2024

By ESG Thread · 31 December 2023

Environmental, social and governance (ESG) factors are becoming increasingly important for all businesses, small ones included, to consider. As we enter 2024, there are several key ESG trends that small business owners should be aware of.

Sustainability reporting

Regulators and investors are demanding more transparency on ESG policies and performance. Large corporations have released sustainability reports for years, and small businesses are starting to follow. Through 2024 we expect this reporting to become more mainstream for smaller companies too. Tracking and disclosing your energy use, carbon emissions, waste and other metrics shows stakeholders you are managing your environmental impact.

Climate risk assessments

With the effects of climate change intensifying, small businesses need to evaluate how rising temperatures, extreme weather and related issues could affect their operations. A climate risk assessment lets you identify vulnerabilities and build resilience plans — and it is increasingly an expected part of due diligence.

Renewable energy adoption

Moving to renewable sources like solar and wind is becoming more affordable and accessible. Many small businesses can cut utility costs by installing rooftop solar or joining a community solar programme through their provider. Using renewables lowers your carbon footprint and signals a real commitment to sustainability.

Ethical supply chain management

Consumers care more than ever about where products come from and whether supply chains are ethical. Small businesses should monitor their suppliers' labour practices, environmental impacts and inclusion efforts — and seek out diverse suppliers as part of an equitable, environmentally responsible chain.

DEI policies and practices

Diversity, equity and inclusion are now central to attracting and retaining talent. Thoughtful DEI policies — equitable hiring and promotion, harassment-free workplaces, flexible working and employee resource groups — should be a core part of your culture.

ESG is here to stay, and small businesses benefit from getting ahead of the trends. Reach out for help developing your ESG strategy.
