Why this matters now

Helping companies take charge of their ESG journey

The ESG landscape is becoming more demanding, with escalating pressures and requirements from public and private actors across every sector of the economy.

The focus has long sat on large, listed organisations. That remains true — but it is widening to take in new sectors, new geographies, and smaller enterprises. More diverse and smaller businesses are now being asked, or required, to examine their ESG impacts and dependencies: sometimes through regulation pulling them into scope, sometimes through information requests and contractual obligations cascading down from larger partners.

The case for acting early

Why care about ESG?

SMEs and start-ups alike will need to integrate ESG and wider sustainability thinking to stay attractive and, over the long term, viable. Your business may not be touched by today's laws, shareholder demands or consumer expectations — but it is likely they will reach you over the coming years.

Not every ESG issue will be relevant to your model. Yet the near-universal advantages of strong practice are real: a stronger brand, better talent attraction and retention, sharper risk management, and access to a wider pool of capital. These benefits, financial and otherwise, tend to outweigh the resources invested to reach them.
